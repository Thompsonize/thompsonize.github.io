Pasta (Red)


This is pure Jersey, reduced down to absolute basics. Feeds 2 for dinner with leftovers, scales easily.

Heat an enameled cast iron pot. Squeeze 3 (ideally local supermarket brand) hot italian sausages out of their casings into the pot. Brown the sausage a little, breaking it apart. Add half a roughly chopped onion. If you want, add some Italian seasoning and/or hot red pepper flakes. You don't need to, it's literally what's inside the hot Italian sausage. I do it because it's fun.

Cook until reduced by maybe 1/3. Drain if you want - sometimes I don't bother, depending on how much fat we're talking about.

Add a large can of crushed (or whatever) San Marzano (or whatever) tomatoes. Mix together. Cover. Reduce heat to low. Simmer for awhile.

Preheat the oven to 325.

Boil some water. Make some rigatoni or ziti or whatever. Cook it shy of al dente.

Strain the pasta and put in a glass loaf pan. Add sauce a little at a time, stirring as you go, until you get the ratio you want. Freeze the rest of the sauce, or keep it in the fridge for a bit with the leftover mozzarella for parms. If your mozzarella is low moisture, shoot for a wetter sauce, otherwise less (or more meaty) parts of the sauce.

Top with grated (or canned) parmesan and sliced mozzarella. Bake for a bit (20ish minutes) to melt cheese, then turn up the oven to 450 for a few minutes to brown it if you want.
