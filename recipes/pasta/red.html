Pasta: Red


This is pure Jersey, reduced down to absolute basics. Feeds 4 minimum for dinner with leftovers, scales easily.

Heat an enameled cast iron pot. Squeeze 2 full tray-length local supermarket brand hot italian sausages (about a pound) out of their casings and into the pot. Brown the sausage a little, breaking it apart. Add half a roughly chopped onion. Add Italian seasoning and hot red pepper flakes.

Cook until reduced by maybe 1/3. Drain if you want - I don't fry the sausage in oil as I break it apart so I usually don't bother, depending on how much fat is in the pan.

Add garlic powder to the pan and scrape around to toast. Add a large can of San Marzano (or whatever) tomatoes. Mix together and scrape up fond. Cover. Reduce heat to low. Simmer for awhile. If you have a not-quite-enough-to-use-alone quantity of leftover tomato sauce in the freezer like I often do, add it into the pot while it simmers.

Preheat the oven to 325.

Add as much Ziti to the pot as looks right, usually somewhere between 4oz-8oz. Add 1/2 cup hot water. Simmer low and slow until pasta is mostly done, about 20 minutes. Check every 5 minutes, adding more water as you need to, 1/2 cup at a time, to keep the sauce from burning and the pasta from sticking.

Take off heat. Add a handful of cubed low moisture mozzarella and stir to combine. Dust the surface with your preferred depth of grated Parmesan.

Bake for 20ish minutes in the preheated oven to melt cheese - if it's too dry, with a lid on to retain moisture; if it's too wet, with the lid off to let it reduce.

Optionally, finish by turning up the oven to 425 for a few minutes if you want a little burned crunch on it. Breadcrumbs and/or ricotta can be added before you broil it but I usually don't bother.
